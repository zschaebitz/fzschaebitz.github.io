About Me

Bilingual, bicultural, and endlessly curious

Hello, I'm Finn Zschaebitz

I'm a Media Studies student at the University of Virginia, expected to graduate Fall 2026. Based in Charlottesville, Virginia, and as a dual US-German citizen, I bring a unique perspective shaped by growing up between two cultures–American and German–with native fluency in both languages.

My educational journey has taken me across continents: from Department of Defense schools in Germany, to Charlottesville High School, to earning my German Abitur (equivalent to an International Baccalaureate) in Business Studies. After community college studies in California and a Computer Science concentration at Piedmont Virginia Community College, I found my home at UVA studying Media Studies.

I'm passionate about the intersection of media, technology, and human connection. Whether it's translation work bridging English and German, creative production with audio and video tools, or exploring how digital media shapes our world, I approach every project with curiosity and a collaborative spirit.

When I'm not studying or working, you can find me ski mountaineering, surfing, or at a live music show.

My Journey

Third-Culture Perspective

Born to a German Air Force pilot and an American nurse practitioner, I grew up navigating both cultures–from DODDS schools in Germany to high school in Virginia. This bicultural upbringing shaped my ability to bridge perspectives and communicate across boundaries.

Global Experience

From volunteering at OSA Conservation in Costa Rica doing eco-farming and sea turtle monitoring, to teaching German at UVA's Summer Language Institute, I seek out experiences that broaden my understanding of the world and connect me with diverse communities.

Collaborative Spirit

Whether working as a Team Lead at Vu Noodles, coordinating telehealth development projects at ThreadEX, or tutoring students in Germany, I thrive in environments where I can help others succeed and find common ground through humor and empathy.
